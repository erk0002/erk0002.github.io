Text of Feedback

Welcome Tristan van Erk,

Thank you for submitting your “Who Am I?” form. The feedback given here is for you to use when you start the program, so you don’t have to respond to it just now.

Reading your motivation, it is clear you have some experience and an interest in developing programs. I am glad to see this, and I am interested in hearing the reason behind your interest. Why do you find these subjects interesting? What do you wish to develop? Is there a challenge you already set for yourself? A strong motivation will help you complete the study program successfully, and overcome times where it will feel difficult or stressful to do so.

When looking at your goals after completing the studies, it is nice to see you are interested in becoming a software engineer. Besides working on code, what else do you enjoy in being a software engineer? Why does it appeal to you more than being a programmer? Are there other options that will allow you to express your creativity? Would you enjoy working in a team? I would recommend you to try and research more about software engineering but maybe other positions as well just to understand your options, what would you like to do in your day to day? How would you like your role to look like? Look more into the program, what interests you in the courses taught. I also recommend you participate in the ICT conference upcoming year where youll meet companies and can get more information about different professions in the ICT field.

Looking at your SWOT analysis, it is noticeable you are aware of one of your dominant strengths which can potentially become a weakness or a threat. The recognition and your existing plan to prevent it from becoming a threat is admirable and beneficial. As you mentioned yourself, breaks and setting limitations to perfectionism will benefit your mental and physical health, as working on the same assignment or task for too long will not give you rest and will potentially lead to a burn out. Aspiring for the best results is respectful, but please do not do it on a personal cost. Take care of yourself, don’t be too harsh on yourself and I recommend preparing mentally for situations where the result will not be exactly what you expect it to be, remember there will be group projects and there will be team members with different mentalities and approaches, its important to discuss and be open about your expectations from each other to prevent it from becoming unpleasant for everyone. I am sure you will put this to good use, and it will benefit you a lot during your studies, but with limitations. Enjoy these four years as well, take breaks, have fun too! Its not less important. Also, as you mentioned yourself youll have multiple sources of help and advice, fellow students, student assistants, lecturers, and your personal study career coach, so please feel free to escalate when needed and consult for solutions.

I have noticed you marked yes in two of the bottom 3 questions, in case you would like a personal conversation with one of the teachers to ask in depth questions let us know by sending an email to daphne.heertjes@hz.nl .

I am looking forward to meeting you this coming year and wish you a lovely summer break!

Shirel.
